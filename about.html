About Acts of Love International

Transforming communities through compassion, inclusion, and sustainable support for over 18 years

Our Story

Founded in 2006 and registered in 2007, Acts of Love International is based at Rhema House in Stockton-on-Tees. Our community has faced some of the toughest social challenges in the UK - poverty, unemployment, crime and poor health. Yet it is also a place of strength, resilience, and rich cultural diversity.

For over 18 years, we've been working to transform these challenges into opportunities for growth and connection. Our neighbourhood is filled with incredible people who inspire us with their strength and resilience every day.

"Our neighbourhood is filled with incredible people who inspire us with their strength and resilience every day."

18 Years of Service

Our Mission

Founded in 2005, Acts of Love International has been at the forefront of community development and poverty alleviation in Stockton-on-Tees and surrounding areas.

We are a registered charity (Charity No. 1119687) set up to support people from ethnic minority backgrounds, living in deprived wards to get access to sport and activities which increase health well-being, mental health, confidence and knowledge and to decrease isolation.

Poverty and discrimination have a massive impact on our members decreasing motivation and positive mental health. Our charity works with the community to improve their quality of life underpinned by activities which develop their skills, sporting ability and talents.

Our Journey

A timeline of our growth and impact over the years

2005

Acts of Love International was founded to address poverty and social challenges in Stockton-on-Tees.

2006

Officially established our community programs and began our work in earnest.

2007

Registered as a charity (Charity No. 1119687) and expanded our services.

2010

Established Rhema House as our central hub for community activities and support services.

2015

Expanded our sports and wellbeing programs to serve more community members.

2020

Adapted our services during the pandemic to continue supporting vulnerable community members.

2024

Celebrating 18+ years of service and continuing to expand our impact in the community.
