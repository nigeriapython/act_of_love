About Acts of Love International

Who We Are

Acts of Love International is both a church and a charity, working side-by-side to address marginalisation and build community. We focus on supporting ethnic minorities, asylum seekers, and disadvantaged white communities facing poverty, poor health, and social exclusion.

We do not require our staff, volunteers, or service users to share our faith—but we do ask that they appreciate and respect the Christian values that guide our mission.

Our Story

Founded in 2006 and registered in 2007, Acts of Love International is based at Rhema House in Stockton-on-Tees. Our community has faced some of the toughest social challenges in the UK—poverty, unemployment, crime, and poor health.

Yet it is also a place of strength, resilience, and rich cultural diversity. For over 18 years, we've been working to transform these challenges into opportunities for growth and connection.

"Our neighbourhood ranks among the most deprived in the UK. Life expectancy is lower than average, incomes are below the national median, and many residents face barriers to accessing support. By offering practical help and a welcoming environment, we're changing lives every day."

Our Work

We run a wide range of services to meet local needs

Foodbank

Open Sunday, Wednesday, and Friday (8am–2pm). Partnered with FareShare Northeast, The Moses Project, and the local community.

ESOL Classes

Helping non-native speakers improve English skills and better integrate into the community.

Sports & Wellbeing

Inclusive sports programs designed with cultural sensitivity, including for women who face barriers to accessing public sports facilities.

Clothing & Essentials

Distribution of clothing and essential items to support those in crisis situations.

Faith & Fellowship

Sunday services, Friday Bible studies, and pastoral care for those seeking spiritual support.

Community Events

Bringing people together across cultures through celebrations, workshops, and gatherings.
